# Email Flow UX Review — AeroLease Wise
## Date: March 10, 2026

---

## Business Flow Analysis

Understanding how the actual payment cycle works is the foundation for evaluating every email trigger.

### The Confirmed Payment Creation Path

Payment records in this system are **auto-generated by the flight school portal**, not manually created by admin. The key code is in `server/routes.ts`, `POST /api/portal/hours`:

1. Flight school submits hours via `POST /api/portal/hours`
2. A `flightHourLogs` record is created with `status: "submitted"`
3. **Immediately in the same request**, a `payments` record is auto-created with:
   - `status: "Pending"`
   - `dueDate`: today + 30 days
   - `amount`: `Math.max(reportedHours, lease.minimumHours) × lease.hourlyRate`
   - `flightHourLogId`: linked to the log that generated it
4. Admins receive an **in-app notification** that hours were logged and an invoice was auto-created

There is also a manual admin path: `POST /api/payments` (admin-only) can create payment records directly, but this is supplementary.

### The Critical Finding on Payment Lifecycle

Because payments are **auto-created the instant hours are logged**, the lifecycle is:

```
School logs hours (portal)
    → flightHourLog created (status: submitted)
    → payment created automatically (status: Pending, dueDate = now + 30 days)
    → admin notified in-app
        → admin reviews and can edit/verify
    → school gets payment due reminder 5 days before dueDate
    → school pays via portal (status → Paid)
    → admin notified in-app
```

The `dueDate` on the payment is set to **30 days from the day hours are logged**, not from the end of the billing month. This is an important nuance — a school that logs hours on the 1st of the month gets 30 days to pay; a school that logs on the 28th also gets 30 days (due date would be the 28th of the following month). The due date is entirely relative to the submission date.

### What "Admin Review" Actually Means

Admin does NOT manually create the payment record. The record exists the moment hours are logged. What admin does:
- Sees the in-app notification about hours logged
- Can edit the payment record (adjust amount, add notes, change status)
- Can verify/dispute the hour log
- The `verifiedHours` field on `flightHourLogs` allows admin to override the reported hours

## Current Email Triggers — Evaluation

### Invite & Onboarding Emails

| Trigger | Assessment | Notes |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| Invite sent to flight school | ✅ Correct | Immediate send, appropriate |
| Invite sent to asset owner | ✅ Correct | Immediate send, appropriate |

### Payment Emails (Scheduled — Daily 8am)

**`payment_due_soon` — Payment due in 4–5 days**

Assessment: ✅ Technically correct, but with a caveat.

The prerequisite (hour logging → payment creation) happens before this fires. By the time the scheduler sees a payment with dueDate 4–5 days away, that payment was auto-created 25 days earlier when the school logged hours. The email is therefore valid: a payment exists, a due date exists, and the school needs to pay.

Caveat: The email references `payment.period` (e.g., "January 2026") and `payment.amount` — both are accurate because they come from the payment record. This is fine.

**`payment_overdue_7` — Payment 7 days past due**

Assessment: ✅ Correct, but admin escalation is missing an email.

The school ignored both the 5-day warning and the due date itself. Sending a 7-day overdue reminder to the school is correct. The system also creates an in-app notification for admins, which is good.

Gap: Admins only get an in-app notification — no email. An admin who doesn't log into the portal daily might not see this for days.

**`payment_overdue_30` — Payment 30 days past due**

Assessment: ✅ Correct, but again admin escalation is in-app only.

By 30 days overdue, this is a serious collections issue. Sending an urgent email to the school is correct. Admin in-app notification is marked "urgent" — but still no admin email. At this severity, admin should be receiving an email, not just an in-app badge.

### Lease Expiry Emails (Scheduled — Daily 8am)

**`lease_expiry_60` — Lease expiring in 60 days**

Assessment: ✅ Correct for the school.

Gap: Admin is not notified. If the school ignores this email and doesn't contact admin, admin has no automated reminder to follow up on renewal. Admin should also receive this notification (email or in-app).

**`lease_expiry_15` — Lease expiring in 15 days**

Assessment: ✅ Correct for urgency level.

Same gap as above: no admin notification. At 15 days, admin absolutely needs to know — especially if no renewal has been arranged. At minimum, an in-app notification for admins should fire here. An email to admin is strongly recommended.

Also: the aircraft owner is not notified of their aircraft's lease expiring. Owners have a portal and would reasonably want to know when their aircraft is about to become un-leased (i.e., revenue stops).

### Monthly Emails (1st of Month — 8am)

**`monthly_hours_reminder` — Reminder to log flight hours**

Assessment: ✅ Correct and necessary. This is the most important proactive trigger in the system.

However, timing has a significant flaw: it fires on the **1st of the month** — which is the first day of the new month, meaning it's a reminder to log hours for the month that just ended. This is fine semantically (log last month's hours now), but it means schools only get **one reminder per month**, at the very start.

Recommendation: A second reminder mid-month (around the 15th) or a few days before month-end (e.g., 25th–27th) would catch schools that missed the 1st-of-month reminder. The current setup means a school that ignores the 1st-of-month email has no further nudge until they've been overdue for 7 or 30 days on a payment — but that payment can't exist yet because they haven't logged hours.

**`owner_monthly_summary` — Owner portfolio summary available**

Assessment: ⚠️ Timing issue / content mismatch.

This fires on the **1st of the month at 8am** — the same time as the hours reminder. At that point, flight schools have just been reminded to log their hours and haven't done so yet. The owner summary email says "your monthly summary is available," but the underlying data (last month's hours, payments) may be incomplete because schools haven't logged yet.

The owner summary should fire later in the month — ideally after a reasonable window for hour logging has passed (e.g., the 10th of the month), so the data it summarizes is actually complete.

## Missing Email Triggers

### 1. No email to admin when hours are logged (HIGH PRIORITY)

When a flight school logs hours, admins receive an **in-app notification** only. There is no email. Admins who don't log into the portal daily could miss that an invoice has been auto-created and is waiting for their review. If admin needs to verify hours or adjust the payment before the school sees it, time matters.

**Recommended addition:** Immediate email to all admins when `POST /api/portal/hours` succeeds, notifying them that hours were logged and an invoice was auto-created.

### 2. No email to school when admin modifies their payment (MEDIUM PRIORITY)

When admin edits a payment via `PUT /api/payments/:id`, the code only notifies the school via in-app notification if the status changes to "Paid". If admin adjusts the amount (e.g., corrects for verified hours differing from reported hours), the school has no way to know their payment amount changed unless they log in.

**Recommended addition:** Email to school when admin makes any material change to their payment record (amount change, status change, notes added).

### 3. No email to admin when payment becomes overdue (HIGH PRIORITY)

The `payment_overdue_7` and `payment_overdue_30` triggers create in-app notifications for admins but send **no email**. Admins handling multiple flight schools may not log in every day. An email to admin when a payment crosses 7 days overdue is a material operational need.

**Recommended addition:** Email to admins (same time as the overdue email to the school) for `payment_overdue_7` and `payment_overdue_30`.

### 4. No email to admin or owner when a lease is expiring (HIGH PRIORITY)

`lease_expiry_60` and `lease_expiry_15` only email the flight school. Admin needs to know too, to proactively manage renewals. The aircraft owner also has a legitimate interest — their revenue stops when the lease ends.

**Recommended additions:**
- Admin email for `lease_expiry_60` and `lease_expiry_15`
- Owner email for `lease_expiry_60` (heads-up, not urgent)
- Owner email for `lease_expiry_15` (action required — revenue impact)

### 5. No end-of-month hours reminder (MEDIUM PRIORITY)

There is one monthly reminder on the 1st. If a school ignores it, there is no follow-up until they are 7 days overdue on a payment — but that payment doesn't exist yet because they haven't logged hours. The 7-day overdue reminder is completely bypassed in this scenario.

**Recommended addition:** A second `monthly_hours_reminder` trigger around the 25th of the month, warning schools to log their hours before month-end or they may be billed at minimum hours.

### 6. No email confirmation to school when payment is marked Paid by admin (LOW PRIORITY)

When admin marks a payment as Paid, the school gets an in-app notification but no email confirmation/receipt. A payment receipt email is a standard expectation in financial workflows.

**Recommended addition:** Email to school when admin marks a payment as "Paid," serving as a payment receipt.

## Addressing the User's Core Question

> "A payment will not become due unless the school logs the hours — so we wouldn't even know until then how much is due on them."

**This concern is partially valid but ultimately resolved by how the code actually works:**

- The user's concern assumed admin manually creates payments after reviewing hour logs. That would make "payment due soon" emails nonsensical (no payment exists yet).
- In reality, **payments are auto-created the instant hours are logged** (`POST /api/portal/hours` creates both the log and the payment in the same transaction).
- So `payment_due_soon` is valid: by the time it fires (25 days after hour logging), the payment has existed for 25 days and has a real amount and due date.

**However**, the user's concern reveals a real gap the system doesn't address: **what if a school never logs hours at all for a given month?**

In that case:
- No `flightHourLog` record exists
- No `payment` record exists
- The `payment_due_soon` / `payment_overdue_*` triggers never fire
- The school gets the `monthly_hours_reminder` on the 1st but nothing else
- The lease's `minimumHours` obligation goes unenforced by the email system

This is the genuine operational risk: a school that simply ignores hour logging faces no payment pressure because there's nothing in the `payments` table to trigger overdue reminders. The only touchpoint is the monthly reminder email.

## Summary of User's Concern Validity

The user's concern — **"payment reminders may fire before a payment even exists"** — is NOT a bug in the current code, because payment creation is automatic and tied to hour logging.

But the concern reveals a **real operational gap** that IS a problem: if a school never logs hours, the entire payment reminder system is bypassed. There is no mechanism to chase a school that simply doesn't submit. The `monthly_hours_reminder` on the 1st is the only safeguard, and it's a single email with no follow-up.

The three most important findings are:

1. **Payments are auto-created on hour submission** — the scheduler's `payment_due_soon` and `payment_overdue_*` triggers are technically valid and fire at the correct time. The user's concern is based on a misread of the flow (assumed manual admin payment creation), but the code auto-creates payments.

2. **Admin has no email escalation path** — when payments go overdue (7 or 30 days), admins only get in-app notifications. No email is sent to admins. In a real operation, this means overdue payments could go unnoticed by admins who don't log in daily.

3. **No hour-logging confirmation email exists** — when a school logs hours, they get no email confirmation that their submission was received and that an invoice of $X has been created, due on [date]. This is a significant gap in the payment communication chain — the school only finds out about their invoice amount by logging into the portal.
